01 · Thesis

Manual workflow, not capital, is the constraint on scaling the GC

LV's growth ceiling today is set by how much paper, email, and back-and-forth a human-shaped back-office can absorb. Pay applications, subcontractor contracts, draws, and field reporting all live in document-and-thread workflows that don't compose.

The investment thesis

The AI Mission Control program is the rebuild, taking each of these flows from chat-and-PDF to a system that proposes, validates, and produces.

The same back-office that today caps LV at ~$50M of construction billing needs to carry $90–100M/yr under the Extended BD plan. Hiring proportionally is not the path. That's the manual scaling that already broke. AI Mission Control is what makes the roster stabilization (Pillar #2 in the SPC update) capable of absorbing the BD pipeline (Pillar #3) without another headcount step-up.

The Ask

Grady sent the team-wide note on April 23 asking every member of the LV org to log their recurring workflows: daily, weekly, or monthly tasks with defined steps, inputs, and outputs.

The Goal

Three things at once: see where the team's time is actually going, identify processes to streamline or automate, and shrink the manual work that caps the GC's throughput.

Where It Feeds

The captured workflows become the backlog Jonah and the engineering bench (Slide 04) prioritize against, and the source list for the Phase 02–04 build sequence (Slide 05).

The Capture Format

Each workflow gets logged with the same structured fields so the inventory is comparable across roles and surfaces the right candidates for automation.

Header Fields
  • Workflow: [Title]
  • Internal Responsible Parties: who owns it
  • Applications Used: Outlook, Box, Excel, Autodesk, Yardi, etc.
  • Frequency: daily / weekly / monthly / as-needed
  • Critical Assumptions: the dependencies that have to hold
Per-Step Detail
  • Step #: Title · Owner · Estimated time
  • Full Step Description
  • Data Input: what comes in, in what format
  • Tech Application Used
  • Output: what gets produced, in what format

Worked Example · From Grady's Note

Workflow
Creation of an Official Subcontractor Change Request Form
Internal Responsible Team Members: Grady (President), Pedro/Greg (PMs), Dorian/Jonah/Lucy (APMs), Alina/Brisa (Project Coordinators)
Applications Used: Outlook, Box, Excel, Box Sign
Frequency: As needed
Type: Cross-functional document workflow with multi-party sign-off, exactly the kind of flow that compresses well under structured automation.
Why this matters for the AI investment: The discipline of "if you catch yourself thinking 'I do this the same way every time,' that's the one to log" produces a credible, evidence-based backlog. We're building Mission Control against the workflows the team actually does, not the ones we assume from the outside. The capture period closes in the next couple of weeks; results feed directly into Phase 02–04 prioritization (Slide 05).

06 · Paths Forward · For Discussion

Two paths · two different investment shapes

Both paths assume the AI build delivers real workflow compression. They differ in how that compressed capability is monetized, and in the size, source, and structure of the capital required. These are presented in parallel as a brainstorm, not a sequence. Steyn is being asked to react to two distinct strategic options.

Path 1

Internal margin lever

Compress LV's own back-office; let the savings drop straight to GC margins as the Extended BD pipeline ramps.

The Investment

Modest, internally funded, focused on the workflows that bottleneck LV today: pay apps, contracts, draws, accounting, reporting. Sized to the Mission Control phase plan; no external capital required.

The Mechanism

A 50% time reduction on non-site processes lets the same back-office team carry roughly 2× the construction billing. Only field bodies (PMs, supers, foremen) scale linearly with project count, because those are real labor, not workflow.

The Outcome

Margin expansion of ~25–40% on the same revenue base, captured directly in LV's GC P&L. Shows up in the SPC scenario tables as a multiplier on whatever construction billing the Extended BD plan lands.

Honest Constraints

The 50% time reduction has to be earned. Only valuable if Extended BD materializes; scaling capacity you don't need is just overhead. Sequencing matters: deploy ahead of the BD ramp.

Path 2

External capital · AI-enabled GC platform

Stand up a sister company funded by outside capital; LV is the lighthouse customer; the platform powers acquisition of margin-squeezed GCs in good markets.

The Investment

Substantial outside capital raise into a sister company. Sized for venture-scale platform build, the engineering bench to run it, and acquisition capacity. LV operates as an anchor / proof customer, potentially as a loss-leader to seed adoption.

The Mechanism

The platform restores margin in acquired GCs that operate in good markets but are seeing margin compression from manual processes. The combination of LV's operating know-how + the AI platform is what AI-only competitors can't replicate. Only LV's existing GC pipeline and team can produce that combination.

The Outcome

Real margin improvement across an acquired portfolio of GCs, with value creation rooted in operational change, not multiple expansion. Improvement is achievable irrespective of valuation environment, which makes the thesis durable across market cycles.

Honest Constraints

Brings outside investors, governance change, and dilution. Requires software / SaaS leadership LV doesn't have in-house today. The integration playbook for acquired GCs has to be built and proven.

How to read these together

These are two different bets, not two stages. Path 1 keeps the value of the AI work internal; it shows up as expanded GC margin in LV's own P&L. Path 2 capitalizes the AI work externally and aims it at a much larger universe of GCs that need the same fix LV does. They differ across capital source, team, governance, timeline, and risk shape. Today's discussion isn't an approval; it's a brainstorm on which direction (or which mix) Steyn wants SPC to lean into when the AI build hits the proof-out milestone.

Discussion question · Path 1

Is the LV margin expansion alone enough to justify the AI investment on a stand-alone basis, independent of any external commercialization?

Discussion question · Path 2

Is the LV operating asset + team something Steyn would want to capitalize externally, and what kind of outside partner would fit the firm's stance on dilution and governance?
